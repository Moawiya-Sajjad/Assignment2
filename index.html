Our Growing World: Population Milestones and Projections

Dive into the dynamic story of our world's population evolution, spanning over five decades. This visualization unveils the ebb and flow of global demographics, shedding light on key milestones and shifts that have shaped our planet's human landscape. From the turning points of the 20th century to the projections for the 21st, witness the fascinating journey of humanity's growth and distribution.

The map paints a tale of global population in 2022. Deeper blue signify dense regions like Asia, especially China and India. Africa varies, with central areas more populated. North America, home to the US and Canada, stands mid-range, while South America displays diversity, spotlighting populous Brazil. Europe shows a west-central density contrasted by quieter east and north. Oceania, especially Australia's heart, remains sparsely populated.

Population by continent (1970-2022)

The line chart shows the trend of population by continents over the years, Asia has been consistently the highest while Europe was second in 1970 and 1980 but dropped to third by Africa, rest have shown just gradual increase.

This stacked bar chart traces population distributions across continents. Asia dominates in size and consistent growth, followed by Africa's recent rapid rise. Europe and North America remain stable, while South America grows steadily. Oceania's subtle increase is dwarfed by larger continents. The chart highlights Asia and Africa's major contribution to the global growth, pushing towards 8 billion by 2022.

Comparison of Area(km²) and Density(per km²) of top 10 countries

Russia leads in area, accounting for 31.78% among the top ten countries. In contrast, Bangladesh, with the smallest landmass, boasts the highest density at 46.01%, reflecting its dense population. Similarly, India and Pakistan have significant densities, indicative of the South Asian population concentration. Brazil, with its vast land, has the least density among these countries.

Progression of Top 10 countries since 1970

The bump chart tracks population changes from 1970 to 2022. It highlights China and India's significant growth, almost converging by 2022, in contrast to countries like USA, Brazil, and Russia.

The slope chart displays difference in top 10 country's population directly from 1970 to 2022. China and India show steep growth, with others like U.S. and Brazil growing at varied rates.
